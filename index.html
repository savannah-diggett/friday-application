Question 01

Which platform do you most enjoy creating content for — LinkedIn, Instagram, or TikTok — and why?

I most enjoy creating content for Instagram because of the flexibility to create both static and dynamic content.

As I'm 23, Instagram has been around for as long as I've been old enough to have social media, so it feels familiar to me. I understand the platform well, and I feel like the content I create naturally resonates with people around my age.

Whereas LinkedIn is (mostly) built around written posts and TikTok is (mostly) centered on video content, Instagram gives me the flexibility to use either format depending on what best suits the message I'm trying to communicate.

It wouldn't make sense to create a dancing TikTok about a serious cybersecurity breach, and it wouldn't make sense to write a lengthy LinkedIn post about your coworker's birthday celebration. On Instagram, however, both topics could work, whether that's through a carousel, a Reel, or a Story. That versatility is what I enjoy most about creating content for the platform.

Question 03

Why is trusted data more valuable than AI?

Savannah Diggett LinkedIn Post · Application

You could drive across the country without a map.

You'd probably stop every now and then to ask for directions. You'd eventually get there… maybe.

Or you could open Google Maps and let it do the work.

Much quicker. Much easier.

But there's one catch. It only works if you put in the right destination.

Type the wrong address, and you'll still get somewhere. Just not where you wanted to be.

AI works in much the same way.

Everyone is focused on getting the latest model, the fastest tools, or the smartest assistant. But AI can only navigate using the information you give it.

If your data is incomplete, inaccurate, or scattered across systems no one understands, don't expect AI to magically point you in the right direction. It'll confidently take you somewhere else.

Good data governance isn't about slowing innovation down, going back to written maps. It's about making sure you've entered the right destination before you hit "Start."

Because the smartest navigation system in the world is only as good as the address you give it.

Note: I used Google Maps as the example here because it's more commonly used than Apple Maps, but the platform itself isn't the point.
